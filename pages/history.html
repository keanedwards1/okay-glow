The Journey of Korean Skincare

A timeline of beauty traditions from ancient wisdom to modern innovation

Goryeo Dynasty

During the Goryeo Dynasty, the noble class developed the first documented skincare rituals. Women used mung beans ground into powder to cleanse their faces, a practice that influenced modern cleansing products.

Early Joseon Dynasty

The Joseon Dynasty saw the creation of detailed beauty guidelines in texts like "Dongui Bogam." Camellia oil and rice water became essential skincare ingredients, laying the foundation for modern K-beauty philosophy.

Traditional Ingredients Era

Ginseng, green tea, and honey emerged as key ingredients in Korean skincare. These natural ingredients were prized for their healing and beautifying properties.

Modern Innovation Begins

Post-liberation Korea saw the emergence of the first modern cosmetic companies, blending traditional wisdom with scientific advancement.

K-Beauty Revolution

The 1990s marked the beginning of the global K-beauty phenomenon, with innovations like sheet masks and BB creams gaining worldwide popularity.

Modern Era

Today's K-beauty industry leads global skincare innovation, combining centuries-old ingredients with cutting-edge technology and sustainable practices.
