The History of Double Cleansing: A Korean Skincare Essential

By Sarah Kim November 21, 2024 5 min read
Back to All Blogs

Double cleansing, a fundamental step in Korean skincare routines, has transformed how we think about facial cleansing. This two-step process, which involves using an oil-based cleanser followed by a water-based cleanser, has been practiced in Korea for centuries.

The Origins of Double Cleansing

The practice of double cleansing can be traced back to the Three Kingdoms period of Korea (57 BC to 935 AD). During this time, women would use plant oils to remove their makeup, followed by rice water to cleanse their skin. This traditional method laid the foundation for modern double cleansing techniques.

Why Two Steps?

The science behind double cleansing is rooted in the principle of "like dissolves like." Oil-based cleansers effectively break down oil-based impurities such as sebum, sunscreen, and makeup. The water-based cleanser then removes water-based impurities like sweat and dirt.

Modern Double Cleansing

Today's double cleansing methods combine traditional wisdom with modern skincare science. Korean beauty brands have developed innovative formulations that make this ancient practice more effective and enjoyable than ever.

How to Double Cleanse: Step-by-Step

Ready to incorporate double cleansing into your routine? Follow these simple steps:

  1. Start with Dry Hands and Face Begin with a completely dry face and clean hands. This ensures the oil-based cleanser can properly break down makeup and sunscreen.
  2. Apply an Oil-Based Cleanser Gently massage it in circular motions to lift away oil-based impurities, makeup, and excess sebum.
  3. Add Water to Emulsify Splash a bit of warm water onto your face, turning the oil into a milky emulsion that helps rinse away grime.
  4. Rinse Thoroughly Wash off the emulsified oil cleanser with lukewarm water until fully removed.
  5. Use a Water-Based Cleanser Apply a gentle water-based cleanser to tackle sweat, dirt, and water-soluble debris.
  6. Massage and Rinse Cleanse for about 30 seconds, then rinse well to leave no residue behind.
  7. Pat Dry and Continue Gently pat your face dry with a soft towel, then proceed with the rest of your skincare routine.

By adding this simple ritual to your skincare routine, you'll create the perfect canvas for the rest of your products. The result is skin that feels fresh, balanced, and glowing.
